When Your Network Holds You Back

In a cloud-first era, legacy wide area network (WAN) configurations restrict business operations. Rigid MPLS architectures are expensive, slow to provision, and struggle with the traffic demands of SaaS and cloud-hosted platforms. Meanwhile, relying on single public broadband lines leaves branches exposed to carrier outages, high packet loss, and jitter that interrupts real-time applications like VoIP and video conferencing.

What is Managed SD-WAN?

Software-Defined Wide Area Networking (SD-WAN) abstracts network control from physical hardware, creating an intelligent routing overlay. This allows you to combine diverse first-mile circuits—like local fiber, dynamic cable, cellular LTE/5G, and Starlink satellite connections—into a single, high-availability transport pipeline.

SD-WAN dynamically monitors traffic conditions across all circuits in real time, automatically steering critical application sessions onto the best available path to prevent disconnects and downtime.

Key Benefits & Capabilities

Increased Productivity

Bond multiple lines active-active. If a primary circuit fails, sessions failover sub-second with zero dropped VoIP calls or database disconnects.

Enhanced Performance

Application-aware routing prioritizes critical business tools (Office365, Zoom, ERP database) over non-essential traffic to optimize bandwidth.

Technology Choice

Vendor-neutral design. Standardize on high-performance Fortinet FortiGate edge components or budget-friendly MikroTik setups.

Reduced TCO

Drastically cut recurring first-mile transport costs by replacing expensive private circuits with managed public broadband links.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does SD-WAN compare to legacy MPLS?

MPLS provides high reliability but at a very high cost per megabit and slow provisioning cycles. SD-WAN delivers equivalent reliability by bonding multiple low-cost broadband lines active-active. By using multiple paths, SD-WAN often delivers less monthly downtime than a single MPLS link at a fraction of the total cost.

Can XTT bond Starlink with terrestrial lines?

Yes. Our SD-WAN edge configurations can bond satellite links (like Starlink) with local cellular LTE/5G or wireline connections. The software dynamically handles the latency differences to ensure stable VPN transit.

What hardware is used at the edge?

We deploy Fortinet FortiGate (40F/60F/80F) appliances for enterprise security mandates and MikroTik devices for cost-conscious SMB branches.
